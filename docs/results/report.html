fact_006
When should complementary foods be introduced to an infant?
Show details

Response:

Complementary foods should be introduced to an infant at 6 months of age (180 days), as recommended by the WHO/UNICEF Infant and Young Child Feeding Guidelines. This is because, by 6 months, breast milk alone no longer meets the infant's iron, zinc, and energy needs, and the infant's digestive system can handle a variety of foods (WHO/UNICEF). Introducing complementary foods earlier or later than 6 months is not recommended, as it can increase the risk of infection or nutrient deficiencies. Breastfeeding should continue along with complementary foods for up to 2 years of age or beyond.
