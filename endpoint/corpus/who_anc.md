# WHO Antenatal Care Recommendations (2016 Model)

**Source:** WHO recommendations on antenatal care for a positive pregnancy experience (2016)
**URL:** https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241549912

## Number of contacts

The WHO 2016 antenatal care (ANC) model recommends a minimum of **8 contacts** during pregnancy to reduce perinatal mortality and improve women's experience of care. This replaces the older Focused Antenatal Care (FANC) model, which recommended 4 visits.

The recommended schedule is:

- **First contact**: in the first trimester (up to and including 12 weeks of gestation)
- **Second contact**: 20 weeks
- **Third contact**: 26 weeks
- **Fourth contact**: 30 weeks
- **Fifth contact**: 34 weeks
- **Sixth contact**: 36 weeks
- **Seventh contact**: 38 weeks
- **Eighth contact**: 40 weeks

If pregnancy continues past 41 weeks, women should be brought to a facility for assessment.

## Why 8 contacts

Evidence reviewed by WHO found that increased contacts (compared to 4-visit FANC) were associated with reduced perinatal deaths and increased maternal satisfaction. More contacts allow for better detection of complications, more opportunities for health promotion (nutrition, danger signs, birth preparedness) and more relationship-building between the pregnant woman and her care providers.

## First contact: priorities

At the first contact (ideally before 12 weeks):
- Confirm pregnancy and estimate gestational age
- Baseline blood pressure, weight, height
- Blood group, haemoglobin, HIV, syphilis, hepatitis B testing
- Urine test for protein and infection
- Start iron and folic acid supplementation
- Counselling on nutrition, danger signs, and birth preparedness
