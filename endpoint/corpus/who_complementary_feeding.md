# Complementary Feeding (6-24 months)

**Sources:**
- WHO Complementary Feeding guidelines
- UNICEF Infant and Young Child Feeding (IYCF) recommendations
- MoHFW IYCF guidelines for India

## When to start

Complementary foods should be introduced when the infant turns **6 months of age** (180 days). This is the recommended starting point for ALL healthy infants. Starting earlier (before 6 months) is generally not recommended — it can displace breast milk and increase risk of infection. Starting later (after 7 months) risks nutrient deficiencies and growth faltering.

Breastfeeding should **continue** alongside complementary foods, up to 2 years of age or beyond.

## Why 6 months

By 6 months:
- Breast milk alone no longer meets the infant's iron, zinc, and energy needs
- The infant's digestive system can handle a variety of foods
- The infant is developmentally ready (can sit with support, has good head control, can move food from front to back of mouth)

## Frequency and amount

Recommendations grow with age:

| Age          | Meals per day                   | Snacks per day | Approx. amount per meal |
| ------------ | ------------------------------- | -------------- | ----------------------- |
| 6-8 months   | 2-3                             | 0-1            | start 2-3 tablespoons, build to ½ cup (125 ml) |
| 9-11 months  | 3-4                             | 1-2            | ½ cup (125 ml)          |
| 12-23 months | 3-4 family meals                | 1-2            | ¾ cup to 1 cup (175-250 ml) |

## What to feed

- **Variety is key.** Include foods from all groups daily: cereals/roots, legumes/nuts, dairy, eggs/meat/fish, vitamin-A-rich fruits and vegetables, other fruits and vegetables.
- **Iron-rich foods** are especially important: eggs, meat, fish, legumes, dark green leafy vegetables, iron-fortified cereals.
- **Texture progression:** start with thick mashed foods at 6 months; progress to chopped finger foods by 9-12 months; family foods (suitably chopped) by 12 months.
- **Hygiene matters.** Wash hands before preparing food and before feeding. Use clean utensils. Discard leftover food after 2 hours at room temperature.

## What NOT to feed

- **Honey** before 12 months (risk of infant botulism)
- **Whole cow's milk** as a main drink before 12 months (poor iron source, can displace breast milk)
- **Sugary drinks, packaged juices, junk food** at any age in this window
- **Whole nuts, hard small items** (choking hazard) before age 4
- **Tea, coffee** (reduce iron absorption)
- **Salt and sugar** should not be added to infant foods

## Common challenges

- **Picky eating** at 9-15 months is normal. Continue offering rejected foods on different days — children often need 8-15 exposures before accepting a new food.
- **Refusing to eat during illness:** continue breastfeeding, offer small frequent feeds, give plenty of fluids. Increase intake during recovery.
- **Slow weight gain:** consult a doctor for assessment. Do not switch to commercial formulas without medical advice.
