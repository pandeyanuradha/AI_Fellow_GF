# MoHFW Anemia Mukt Bharat — Iron and Folic Acid Supplementation

**Source:** Anemia Mukt Bharat (AMB) operational guidelines, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India
**URL:** https://anemiamuktbharat.info/

## Pregnant women

**Daily dose during pregnancy:** 1 IFA red tablet daily, containing:
- **60 mg of elemental iron**
- **500 micrograms (mcg) of folic acid**

**Duration:** at least **180 days** during pregnancy, starting from the second trimester (after 12 weeks of gestation) when nausea has typically subsided. If started later, continue for the remainder of pregnancy.

**Postpartum:** Continue daily IFA supplementation for **180 days** after delivery, especially for lactating women.

## Counselling points

Pregnant women should be told:
- Take the IFA tablet with food or after a meal to reduce stomach upset
- Do not take with tea, coffee, or milk (these reduce iron absorption)
- Taking with vitamin C-rich foods (citrus, amla) improves absorption
- Side effects of black stools, mild nausea, and constipation are normal and not harmful
- If severe vomiting or diarrhoea occurs, consult the health worker — do not stop on your own

## Children 6-59 months

Bi-weekly IFA syrup, 1 ml twice a week containing 20 mg iron + 100 mcg folic acid. Continue for the whole year except during acute illness.

## Adolescents (10-19 years)

Weekly IFA tablet (blue tablet for school-going children, pink for adolescent girls and women of reproductive age) containing:
- 60 mg of elemental iron
- 500 mcg of folic acid

One tablet per week.

## Severe anemia

- Hb < 7 g/dL or symptomatic anemia: requires medical evaluation, possible IV iron or transfusion. **Refer to a doctor — do not self-treat with higher oral doses.**
- Hb 7-10.9 g/dL: therapeutic dose 2 IFA tablets daily for 3 months, then prophylactic dose. Under medical supervision.

## Important safety notes

- **Iron overdose** is a serious risk in young children. Keep tablets out of reach.
- Do **not** double-up on missed doses. Resume the normal schedule.
- IFA supplementation in pregnancy is preventive; it does **not** replace screening and treatment of anemia.
