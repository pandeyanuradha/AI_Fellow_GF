# Pregnancy Danger Signs (WHO + MoHFW)

**Sources:**
- WHO Pregnancy, Childbirth, Postpartum and Newborn Care guide
- MoHFW Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) IEC materials

These are signs that warrant **immediate** medical attention. The woman should be taken to a hospital or health facility right away. In India, call **108** (emergency services) or visit the nearest 24-hour Primary Health Centre (PHC) / Community Health Centre (CHC).

## During pregnancy

- **Vaginal bleeding** of any amount
- **Severe headache** that does not improve with rest
- **Blurred vision** or seeing spots (signs of pre-eclampsia)
- **Convulsions (fits) or loss of consciousness** (signs of eclampsia)
- **Severe abdominal pain**
- **High fever** (above 38°C / 100.4°F)
- **Severe vomiting** that prevents eating or drinking
- **Difficulty breathing** or chest pain
- **Reduced or no foetal movement** after 28 weeks
- **Sudden swelling** of face, hands or feet (especially with headache or visual changes)
- **Watery discharge** from the vagina (possible rupture of membranes)
- **Severe pallor or weakness** that prevents normal activity

## During labour

- Bleeding more than a heavy period
- Continuous severe pain between contractions
- Labour lasting more than 12 hours in a first pregnancy (or 8 hours in subsequent)
- Cord prolapse (umbilical cord visible at the vagina before the baby)
- Convulsions

## Postpartum (within 6 weeks of delivery)

- Heavy bleeding (soaking a pad in less than an hour)
- Foul-smelling vaginal discharge
- Fever above 38°C
- Severe headache or visual changes
- Severe abdominal pain
- Breast pain or swelling with fever (mastitis)
- Pain or swelling in the calves (possible deep vein thrombosis)
- Feeling extremely sad or having thoughts of harming yourself or the baby (postpartum depression — please reach out to a doctor or call MoHFW helpline 1097)

## Newborn danger signs (first 28 days)

- Not feeding well or refusing feeds
- Fever or low body temperature
- Fast or difficult breathing
- Convulsions
- Lethargy or unresponsiveness
- Yellow skin / eyes (jaundice) extending to palms and soles
- Persistent vomiting or diarrhoea
- Umbilical stump red, swollen or with pus

**Any** of these signs in a newborn is a reason to seek immediate medical care.

## Why this matters

Most maternal and newborn deaths occur because danger signs are recognised too late or because the family delays seeking care. Knowing these signs — and acting on them immediately — saves lives.
