# India Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) Schedule

**Source:** Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India — National Immunization Schedule
**URL:** https://nhm.gov.in/

## At birth (within 24 hours, or as soon as possible)

- **BCG** (Bacillus Calmette-Guérin, against tuberculosis) — single dose, intradermal, left upper arm.
  - If missed at birth: can be given **up to 1 year of age**.
- **OPV-0** (Oral Polio Vaccine, zero dose) — 2 drops oral.
- **Hepatitis B birth dose** — 0.5 ml intramuscular, anterolateral thigh.

## 6 weeks

- **OPV-1**, **Pentavalent-1** (DPT + HepB + Hib), **fIPV-1** (fractional inactivated polio), **Rotavirus-1**, **PCV-1** (pneumococcal)

## 10 weeks

- OPV-2, Pentavalent-2, Rotavirus-2

## 14 weeks

- OPV-3, Pentavalent-3, fIPV-2, Rotavirus-3, PCV-2

## 9-12 months

- **Measles-Rubella (MR) 1st dose**
- **JE vaccine 1st dose** (in JE-endemic districts)
- **PCV booster**

## 16-24 months

- MR 2nd dose, DPT booster-1, OPV booster, JE 2nd dose, Vitamin A 2nd dose

## 5-6 years

- DPT booster-2

## 10 years, 16 years

- Td (tetanus and diphtheria) booster

## Pregnancy

- 2 doses of **Td** (tetanus and adult diphtheria), at least 4 weeks apart. First dose as early as possible; second dose at least 4 weeks before delivery.

## BCG specifics

- Given as a single 0.05 ml dose (newborn) or 0.1 ml (older infant), intradermal.
- A small papule usually appears at the injection site 2-3 weeks later, scabs over, and forms a small scar by 6-12 weeks. This is the expected response.
- Contraindications: known HIV infection with significant immunosuppression, generalised skin disease at the injection site.

## If a child has missed vaccines

Catch-up schedules are available — consult the nearest health centre. Do **not** try to calculate doses yourself.
